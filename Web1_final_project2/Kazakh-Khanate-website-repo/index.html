Kazakh khans

Janibek Khan

Reign: 1465–1473 Contributions: Co-founder of the Kazakh Khanate with Kerei Khan. United various nomadic tribes to establish the foundation of the Kazakh people. Promoted peace and stability among the tribes, laying the groundwork for the Khanate’s future development. 😀

Kerei Khan

Kasym Khan

History of the Kazakh Khanate (1465–1847)

The Kazakh Khanate was a nomadic empire that existed from the 15th to the 19th centuries in Central Asia. It was formed in 1465 by Janibek Khan and Kerei Khan, who broke away from the Uzbek Khanate. The Khanate reached its peak under Kasym Khan in the 16th century, expanding its territory and establishing trade routes with Russia and China. However, the Khanate faced numerous challenges, including invasions from the Oirats and Dzungars, internal strife, and the growing influence of the Russian Empire. By the 18th century, the Khanate had weakened and fragmented into three "jüz" or hordes: the Great, Middle, and Little Hordes. These hordes eventually lost their independence and were absorbed into the Russian Empire in the 19th century.
